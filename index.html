The shared architecture

In differential geometry, a metric on a 4-manifold produces the Hodge star operator — an involution whose ±1 eigenspaces decompose curvature into self-dual and anti-self-dual sectors. Integration over the manifold yields the signature: a topological invariant.

In number theory, a norm form on a quadratic field produces Galois conjugation — an involution whose spectral trace at each prime is the quadratic character χ(p) = ±1. The Euler product assembles this prime-by-prime data into the Dedekind zeta function, and the class number formula extracts a discrete invariant.

The objects are entirely different. But the mechanism is the same.

Quadratic form → Involution → Local spectral data → Global invariant

In geometry, this always works: the Hodge star produces a genuine eigenspace decomposition Λ² = Λ²₊ ⊕ Λ²₋. In arithmetic, it works only when the algebra splits.

Split prime (χ = +1)

K ⊗ ℚp ≅ ℚp × ℚp
  • Product of fields → idempotents exist
  • e₁ = (1,0), e₂ = (0,1)
  • Galois involution swaps factors
  • Genuine ±1 eigenspace decomposition

Inert prime (χ = −1)

K ⊗ ℚp ≅ 𝔽p²
  • Remains a field → no nontrivial idempotents
  • e² = e ⟹ e = 0 or e = 1
  • Involution exists but can't be diagonalised
  • Eigenspaces remain coupled
Quadratic reciprocity governs precisely when the local algebra splits — when the involution can be realised as a genuine projector decomposition. The Legendre symbol (−3/p) is the arithmetic test for diagonalisability.

The Eisenstein integers: every step explicit

ℤ[ω] with ω = e2πi/3 is the minimal working example. The norm a² − ab + b² defines the involution σ(ω) = ω̅. The quadratic character χ₋₃(p) classifies every prime. Here is the local spectral data, computed directly:

■ Split: p ≡ 1 (mod 3), χ = +1 ■ Inert: p ≡ 2 (mod 3), χ = −1 ■ Ramified: p = 3

Each split prime contributes two independent linear factors to the Euler product. Each inert prime contributes one quadratic factor. The Euler product over all primes assembles into ζℚ(ω)(s) = ζ(s) · L(s, χ₋₃), and the class number formula yields hK = 1: perfect global balance.

Two assembly mechanisms

The local spectral data is the same kind of thing in both settings: ±1 eigenvalues of an involution generated by a quadratic form. But the way this data is collected globally is fundamentally different:

Additive Assembly

τ(M) = 1⁄12π² ∫M (‖F₊‖² − ‖F₋‖²)
  • Integration sums local curvature
  • Hirzebruch signature theorem
  • Output: signature τ(M) ∈ ℤ
  • Measures net self-dual asymmetry

Multiplicative Assembly

L(1, χ) = 2π hK⁄wK√|D|
  • Euler product multiplies local factors
  • Class number formula
  • Output: class number hK ∈ ℤ
  • Measures factorisation obstruction
For ℚ(ω), hK = 1. In geometric language, this is like a manifold whose signature vanishes — the local spectral contributions balance perfectly. There is no net obstruction.

Why Gaussian and Eisenstein?

Among all imaginary quadratic fields ℚ(√D), exactly two are simultaneously cyclotomic, Euclidean, equipped with extra units, and realised by maximally symmetric planar lattices:

Gaussian ℤ[i]

N = a² + b²
  • D = −1, discriminant −4
  • Square lattice, 4-fold symmetry
  • Units: {±1, ±i}
  • Split primes: p ≡ 1 (mod 4)

Eisenstein ℤ[ω]

N = a² − ab + b²
  • D = −3, discriminant −3
  • Hexagonal lattice, 6-fold symmetry
  • Units: {±1, ±ω, ±ω²}
  • Split primes: p ≡ 1 (mod 3)

These are the two maximally symmetric quadratic geometries. In both, the full pipeline — quadratic form → involution → spectral data → global invariant — is completely explicit.

The Unifying Principle

Quadratic-involutive architecture

The parallel lies not in identifying geometric and arithmetic objects, but in the shared mechanism by which local spectral information produces global invariants:

Quadratic form ⟹ Involution ⟹ Local ±1 data ⟹ Global invariant

In geometry, metricity generates chirality, chirality generates curvature eigenvalues, integration yields the signature. In arithmetic, the norm generates conjugation, conjugation generates quadratic characters, the Euler product yields the class number. The quadratic form is the source of all subsequent structure.
